# Ginko Content Strategy

## Overview

This document defines our content strategy for the Ginko marketing site blog. Our goal is to establish Ginko as a thought leader in AI-assisted development while providing genuine value to developers and engineering leaders.

**Philosophy:** Stock and Flow
- **Stock content**: Evergreen pieces that remain valuable over time
- **Flow content**: Timely pieces that address current trends and conversations

---

## Audience Profiles

### Primary: Individual Developers
**Who:** Software engineers using AI coding assistants daily
**Pain points:**
- Repeating context every session
- AI forgetting previous decisions
- Losing flow state when switching tasks
- No persistence between AI conversations

**Content preferences:**
- Practical, actionable advice
- Code examples and demos
- Developer-to-developer tone
- Concise, scannable format

### Secondary: Engineering Managers & Tech Leads
**Who:** Leaders responsible for team productivity and tooling decisions
**Pain points:**
- No visibility into AI-assisted work
- Knowledge silos across team members
- Difficulty measuring AI tool ROI
- Onboarding friction for new hires

**Content preferences:**
- Strategic perspectives
- ROI and productivity metrics
- Team coordination strategies
- Case studies and outcomes

## Post Formats

### 1. Problem/Solution Posts (Stock)
**Length:** 1000-1500 words
**Structure:**
- Hook: Relatable problem statement
- Problem deep-dive: Why this matters
- Solution approach: How to fix it
- Practical application: Steps to implement
- Subtle CTA: How Ginko helps

**Example:** "Why AI Assistants Forget Everything (And How to Fix It)"

### 2. How-To Guides (Stock)
**Length:** 800-1200 words
**Structure:**
- Goal statement: What you'll accomplish
- Prerequisites: What you need
- Step-by-step instructions
- Expected outcomes
- Next steps

**Example:** "Back in Flow in 30 Seconds: The ginko start Experience"

### 3. Pattern Posts (Stock)
**Length:** 600-1000 words
**Structure:**
- Pattern name and context
- When to use it
- How it works
- Code/workflow examples
- Related patterns

**Example:** "Patterns and Gotchas: Teaching Your AI Partner"

### 4. Thought Leadership (Flow)
**Length:** 800-1200 words
**Structure:**
- Provocative thesis
- Supporting arguments
- Industry implications
- Call to conversation

**Example:** "The End of Context Rot: What AI Development Looks Like in 2026"

### 5. Case Studies (Stock)
**Length:** 1000-1500 words
**Structure:**
- Challenge faced
- Approach taken
- Results achieved
- Lessons learned
- Applicability to readers

## Content Guidelines

### Voice & Tone
- **Collegial:** Developer-to-developer, not vendor-to-customer
- **Helpful:** Genuine value, not thinly-veiled sales pitches
- **Confident:** We know this space well
- **Approachable:** No jargon walls or gatekeeping

### CTA Placement
- **Primary CTA:** End of post, after providing value
- **Style:** Subtle invitation, not hard sell
- **Example:** "If context rot is slowing down your team, Ginko can help. Try it free."

### SEO Basics
- Target 1-2 primary keywords per post
- Use descriptive, keyword-rich titles
- Include meta descriptions (150-160 chars)
- Use headers (H2, H3) for structure
- Internal link to related posts

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Provides genuine value without Ginko
- [ ] Readable in under 5 minutes
- [ ] Has clear takeaways
- [ ] Includes practical examples
- [ ] CTA is subtle and appropriate
- [ ] Proofread for clarity and errors
